FAQ

Common Questions

How much does fridge repair cost in Singapore?
Diagnostic fee is $35–$100 (waived if you proceed). Minor repairs (thermostat, door seal) run $150–$250. Major issues (compressor, inverter, PCB) can be $400–$800+. If the fridge is over 8–10 years old and repair exceeds $400–$500, replacement is often more economical.
My washing machine won't spin — is the motherboard worth replacing?
PCB/motherboard replacement for Samsung or LG front-load washers typically costs $250–$500. If the washer is under 6 years old, repair usually makes sense. For machines over 10 years old, consider replacement.
Do you service all HDB towns — Jurong, Woodlands, Tampines, Sengkang, Punggol?
Yes — providers cover islandwide Singapore including all HDB towns, BTO estates, and private condos. Some charge a small surcharge ($10–$20) for restricted access areas like Orchard, CBD, or Sentosa.
My fridge is not cooling and food is spoiling — can I get same-day repair?
Many providers offer same-day or next-day service. Call before 10 AM for the best chance. A $40–$100 diagnostic can save $200–$500 in wasted food. Emergency after-hours call-outs may carry a $30–$80 surcharge.
What brands do you repair?
Technicians service all major brands: Samsung, LG, Panasonic, Haier, Electrolux, Bosch, Beko, Miele, Sharp, Toshiba, and Hitachi. OEM centres charge higher diagnostic fees versus independents.
My gas hob won't ignite — is this dangerous?
A faulty gas hob can be a safety hazard due to gas leakage risk. Always use an EMA-licensed technician. Gas hob repair ranges $80–$250 depending on whether it's a regulator, igniter, or burner issue.
Is there a warranty on appliance repairs?
Most providers offer 30 days to 6 months warranty on repairs and replaced parts. Always ask for a receipt and warranty document before the technician leaves.
